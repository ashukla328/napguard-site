FAQ

Clear boundaries matter. NapGuard is a focused validation concept, not a finished medical product.

Is NapGuard a medical device?

No. This concept is positioned as a consumer nap aid and anti-oversleep product. It does not diagnose, treat, or monitor medical conditions.

Is it a full-night sleep tracker?

No. NapGuard is designed around short naps and oversleep prevention, not overnight reports or long-term sleep dashboards.

Does it detect exact sleep stages?

No. The concept focuses on estimating likely sleep onset for naps. It does not claim perfect sleep-stage accuracy.

Why not just use an alarm?

Alarms start before sleep. If it takes you 15 minutes to fall asleep, the timing no longer matches the nap you intended.

Why a mask?

A mask is less bulky than a headband and more direct than a watch for eye/forehead-adjacent nap signals.

Who is it for?

Students, shift workers, travelers, founders, and anyone who wants a short reset without risking a three-hour accidental sleep.
