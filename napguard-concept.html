Smart nap mask concept

Take a nap without accidentally sleeping for 3 hours.

NapGuard is a lightweight, fabric-first smart sleep mask for controlled power naps. It estimates likely sleep onset with low-profile EEG/EOG-style sensing, then starts the nap timer when rest actually begins.

Concept prototype only. NapGuard is positioned as a consumer nap aid, not a medical device, full-night sleep tracker, meditation headband, or bulky EEG product.

Premium mask first, electronics second.

The form factor is intentionally close to a normal nap mask: soft, light, flexible, and easy to wear on a couch, library chair, dorm bed, flight, or commuter train.

NapGuard uses a gently curved eye-mask silhouette with a soft outer textile shell and a cushioned inner eye cup. The mask body is wider across the eyes and narrower at the nose bridge, with light ventilation channels along the lower edge so it feels less hot during a short nap.

Thin conductive sensor pads are embedded inside the upper forehead edge rather than exposed as a separate headband. Near-eye EOG-style zones sit toward the outer eye and temple region, where they can observe eye-movement patterns while remaining hidden behind fabric.

A shallow electronics pod sits above the nose bridge in this concept, though a side-pod variant is also viable. The module contains the processor, Bluetooth radio, IMU, rechargeable battery, haptic motor driver, small status LED, and optional start/stop button. A soft elastic strap adjusts at the back and avoids the all-around rigid feel of a full sensor headband.

Labeled component layout.

The diagram shows the hidden functional map behind the mask: sensing along the upper edge, eye movement sensing near the outer eyes, and a compact electronics zone above the nose bridge.

EEG sensor pads: thin, soft conductive pads along the upper inside edge estimate likely sleep onset without a separate headband.
EOG/eye movement zones: temple-side areas help detect eye movement patterns associated with settling into sleep.
Processor/Bluetooth: compact pod manages nap mode, phone pairing, and local sensor processing.
Battery and port: small rechargeable cell with low-profile USB-C or magnetic charging access.
Vibration motor: quiet wake cue embedded away from the eyelid pressure zone.
Comfort padding: vented cushion layer keeps contact soft and reduces heat buildup.

How NapGuard works.

The product promise is simple: nap timing should begin when the user likely falls asleep, not when they lie down and hope for the best.

01

Start nap mode

User taps the mask button or starts a nap from the phone. The timer waits in a settling phase.

02

Watch for sleep onset

EEG/EOG-style signals plus motion cues estimate when the user likely transitions from awake to asleep.

03

Begin actual nap timer

A 20-minute nap can begin after probable sleep onset, preserving the intended rest window.

04

Wake gently

Silent haptics help avoid oversleeping without forcing a loud phone alarm in shared spaces.

Exploded layer concept.

NapGuard’s construction keeps sensing and electronics close to the mask body while preserving the feel of soft consumer sleepwear.

Outer fabric layer

Durable matte textile shell, slightly structured to hide components and resist wrinkling in a backpack.

Flexible sensor layer

Printed conductive traces and soft electrode pads routed along the upper edge and near the outer-eye region.

Electronics layer

Thin rigid-flex island for battery, IMU, processor, Bluetooth, LED, button, haptic driver, and charging contacts.

Inner cushion layer

Soft breathable foam around the eye cups, with ventilation breaks to reduce heat during short naps.

Different from alarms and headbands.

NapGuard is intentionally narrow: it is a nap-specific oversleep prevention product, not a full sleep lab on the face.

Normal alarm

Starts counting when the user lies down, so a “20-minute nap” may become five minutes of real sleep after a long wind-down.

Bulky EEG headband

Can feel technical, medical, or uncomfortable for a student trying to nap between classes or while traveling.

NapGuard

Looks like a premium sleep mask, focuses on likely sleep onset, and uses a gentle haptic wake cue to avoid accidental multi-hour naps.
